# Fabrication Plan

## Shop Assumptions

- Cold-rolled steel blanks are cut by laser or fine plasma.
- Boxes are folded on a finger brake and TIG welded at seams.
- Tongue cuts are made before folding, with root radii preserved.
- Tuning uses files, small grinders, and removable test masses.

## Prototype Sequence

1. Cut three tongue coupons: C3, F#3, and B3.
2. Cut and fold three prototype boxes using the same notes.
3. Weld seams with short tacks first, then stitch TIG to minimize warping.
4. Deburr tongue slots without rounding the root radius smaller.
5. Measure raw pitch and sustain before any finish.
6. Tune prototypes and compare measured length corrections to the Wolfram model.
7. Update the CAD length table.
8. Cut the remaining nine boxes.
9. Tune each box off the rack.
10. Mount boxes on isolated rack pads and recheck cross-talk.

## Welding

Use small TIG tacks at closure tabs and corner seams. Avoid heat near tongue
roots; top-face warp changes root stiffness and invalidates the model.

## Finish

Do not powder coat the first prototype. Use light oil or clear wax after
measurement so mass and damping changes remain small and reversible.
